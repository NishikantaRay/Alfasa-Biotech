For Unexplained Female Infertility Management


Unexplained Female Infertility cases in which the standard infertility testing has not found a cause for the failure to get pregnant. Unexplained Female infertility is also referred to as idiopathic infertility. Another way to explain it is the “doctors can’t figure it out” group. There are various causes of Unexplained Female infertility like Insufficient folliculogenesis and nourishment, Poor quality of cervical mucus, poor gonadal functions, overweight, vaginal acidic medium, stress factor, age factor and due to sexual transmitted diseases

A common condition, female infertility is an inability to get pregnant and have a successful pregnancy. This is typically diagnosed after a woman has tried to get pregnant (through unprotected sex) for 12 months without a pregnancy. There are many treatment options for infertility, including medications to correct hormonal issues.
